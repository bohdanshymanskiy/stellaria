Important Safety Information Scroll ▼
Boxed Warning

Ion drive co-exposure and cardiac arrhythmia. Concurrent ion drive co-exposure within 6 hours of Stellaria administration has been associated with prolonged QT interval and, in rare cases, Torsades-class arrhythmia during cryo-induction. Do not administer within 6 hours of active ion drive proximity (≤ 40 m, shielded), or within 12 hours (unshielded). Obtain a baseline ECG prior to first dose.

Indication

Stellaria is indicated as hibernation-assist therapy in licensed deep-space personnel ≥ 21 years undergoing planned cryo-induction on missions of 90 days or longer.

Contraindications

Known hypersensitivity to morphanidine-C; active cryo-sensitization syndrome (CSS); uncorrected Grade II telomeric instability; Class-III temporal stabilizers; pregnancy.

Warnings and Precautions

See Boxed Warning. Hypothalamic set-point drift reported during first-exposure inductions; monitor core temperature for 90 minutes post-thaw. Transient orientation loss may occur on re-animation; no EVA duties for 120 minutes post-thaw.

Adverse Reactions

Most common (≥ 5%): dry mouth (11%), mild paresthesia (9%), transient temporal displacement (7%), metallic taste (6%), mild photosensitivity (5%).

Drug Interactions

Avoid Class-III temporal stabilizers and MAOIs within 14 days. Caffeine analogs > 400 mg/day caf-eq may blunt induction onset.

Use in Specific Populations

Pregnancy: contraindicated. Lactation: not recommended. Pediatric (< 21 y): not established. Hepatic/renal impairment: reduce dose 50% in moderate; contraindicated in severe.
